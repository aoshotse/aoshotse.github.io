I am an Assistant Professor of Organizations and Management at the Goizueta Business School at Emory University. Currently, I also serve as a scientific advisor at Instill Labs. I graduated with a PhD in Organizational Behavior from the Stanford Graduate School of Business. My research examines how people evaluate cultural artifacts and practices. Such evaluations present as single overall impressions, but I show they are driven by particular constituents of the objects being judged. I recover those constituents by decomposing objects usually treated as indivisible and measuring their composition independently of how they are received. My work concentrates on culture and the creative industries, and combines computational analysis of digital trace data with field and archival data. [You can read more about my research here](/research/).

During my time at the GSB, I was a member of the Computational Culture Lab, which aims to pioneer novel ways of unearthing and systematically measuring culture while modeling its evolution. Prior to my studies at Stanford, I obtained an M.S. in Quantitative Methods from the University of Pennsylvania's Graduate School of Education and worked as a research analyst in the Capital Markets function at the Federal Reserve Bank of New York.
